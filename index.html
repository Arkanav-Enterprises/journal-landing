FAQ

Questions, answered.

How does Journal Lock block apps until I journal?

Journal Lock uses Apple's Screen Time framework to lock the apps you choose at a daily time you set. Open a locked app and you'll see a shield asking you to write first. Finish a short entry (as little as ten words) and every locked app unlocks until your next lock time. Learn more →

Is Journal Lock a good app to stop doomscrolling?

Yes, that's exactly what it's for. Instead of just blocking feeds, Journal Lock replaces the scroll urge with a two-minute journaling ritual that breaks the autopilot loop. You still get your apps back every day; you just check in with yourself first. Learn more →

Can it block Instagram Reels, TikTok, and YouTube Shorts?

Journal Lock can lock any app on your iPhone: Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, games, anything. Short-form video apps are the most common thing our users lock. Learn more →

Is my journal private? Can I put a passcode on it?

Every entry is stored locally on your device: no cloud uploads, no accounts, no data mining, no ads. You can also set a separate passcode so the journal itself stays locked from anyone who picks up your phone. Learn more →

Does journaling actually help with anxiety and focus?

Research on "affect labeling" shows that putting feelings into words measurably calms the brain's stress response. Consistent journaling is linked to reduced anxiety, better focus, and stronger emotional resilience. Journal Lock's job is making sure you actually do it. Learn more →

Is Journal Lock free?

Journal Lock is free to download and use. Journal Lock Pro is an optional subscription ($5.99/week or $79.99/year with a 3-day free trial) for unlimited entries, premium themes, and advanced features. Learn more →

How do streaks and the mascot work?

Every day you journal extends your streak and feeds your sprout mascot, which gains health and levels up. Miss a day and it starts to shrink. It's a small thing, but it makes the habit stick. Learn more →

Can I use it as an app blocker for studying?

Absolutely. Set your lock time before your study block and your distracting apps stay locked until you've journaled; many students use the entry itself to plan the session. Learn more →
